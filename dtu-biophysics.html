DTU Biophysics and Fluids Department

Nature optimizes stingers for penetration, but repeated use causes wear. This research introduces Time as a variable to investigate how material properties and mechanical stress degrade "perfect" biological shapes.

Goal Reduce Weight & Increase Range
Software/Hardware MATLAB, Plaster Vibration Machine, Rasberry Pi
The Problem

Biological stingers are geometrically optimized for penetration, but their effectiveness is compromised by mechanical wear over time. Current research defines their ideal "starting" shape, but there is a lack of quantitative data on how repeated collisions and material properties influence structural degradation and functional lifespan.

Key Requirements
  • Modified MATLAB scripts to binarize shadow images and extract sub-pixel tip coordinates for high-precision tracking.
  • Superimposed geometric profiles at exponential time intervals to visualize the transition from sharp to blunt.
  • Utilized a pre-set vibration plaster machine to provide consistent mechanical stress across all material trials.
  • Applied linear regression to log-log plots of mass and tip loss to derive decay constants for different materials.
