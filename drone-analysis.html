Drone Analysis

A simulation study of a quadcopter drone frame evaluating structural strength, vibration behavior, and aerodynamic performance.

Goal To validate the structural and dynamic feasibility of a quadcopter frame through finite element and CFD simulations.
Software SolidWorks, ANSYS
The Problem

Drone frames experience continuous motor-induced vibration in addition to mechanical and aerodynamic loads. Even when a structure is statically strong, resonance near operating frequencies can lead to instability, fatigue, and failure. This project examined whether a proposed frame design could remain structurally and dynamically reliable under flight conditions.

Key Requirements
  • Withstand takeoff and landing loads below material limits
  • Maintain low deformation at motor mounts and joints
  • Compare structural modes to motor operating frequencies
  • Support stable airflow with low drag and sufficient lift

The Prototype

Validated the frame against takeoff and landing impacts and maintained a Factor of Safety of 15, proving the structure is highly robust against mechanical failure.

Detected a critical resonance risk at 244.5 Hz, which sits dangerously close to the motor's operating frequency of 238.7 Hz. This analysis is vital for preventing flight instability and fatigue.

Confirmed aerodynamic efficiency with a 0.25 drag coefficient and 222N of lift. The simulation ensures the frame provides stable airflow and can handle aggressive maneuvers or extra payload.

Simulated heat dissipation from the electronics and motors. Results showed a stable temperature gradient (25°C to 50°C), confirming the material handles internal thermal loads without inducing significant thermal stress or deformation.
