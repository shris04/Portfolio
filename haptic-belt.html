Wearable haptic navigation belt

A wearable electronic travel aid that uses a camera and a haptic belt to communicate surroundings through touch. By utilizing YOLOv5 object detection to identify obstacles in real-time, the system provides tactile vibrations that guide users through complex environments.

Goal Instill safety and confidence in users, providing a more intuitive navigation experience than traditional tools.
Software YOLOv5 object detection, Roboflow
The Problem

Visually impaired individuals often struggle with urban navigation because traditional tools, like the white cane, do not provide comprehensive spatial awareness or obstacle identification. Existing electronic aids are frequently too expensive or complex, leaving millions without the tools needed to navigate safely and confidently.

Key Requirements
  • Safe testing for visually impaired users
  • Real-time obstacle detection
  • Repeatable and standardized evaluation protocol
  • Robust detection across lighting + environments
